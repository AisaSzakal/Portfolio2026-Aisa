Posters

These projects explore different ways of communicating stories through design. Sometimes that form becomes a poster, sometimes a magazine, sometimes a booklet. While the formats differ, they all begin with the same question: how can a story, or a piece of information, be translated into something people can see, understand, and perhaps engage with?
While the formats vary, each project required balancing research, visual hierarchy, and storytelling. Together, they reflect my interest in how ideas travel from concept to communication and how visual design can shape how they are received.

Category Poster Design, Editorial Design, Information Design
Year 2024 – 2026
Tools Illustrator, Photoshop, InDesign

Across these projects, I worked as a researcher, writer, and designer. Depending on the format,
I was responsible for developing concepts, structuring content, creating layouts, and translating
ideas into visual narratives.

These projects range from posters and publications to editorial and information design. Each one began with a different subject, audience, or objective, but all required balancing storytelling, testing, brainstorming, visual hierarchy, and communication.

  • Poster design for Breda in collaboration with Handpicked Breda.
  • Editorial magazine development, including research, interviews, writing, editing, and design.
  • Infographic poster design for an AI-assisted equine integration project.

A collection of posters, publications, and visual experiments. While each project explores a different subject, they all share an interest in translation: turning ideas, stories, and observations into something that can be seen, understood, and experienced by others.
