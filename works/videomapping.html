Videomapping

I first met with the concept of videomapping during my Erasmus semester in Portugal. Since then, I have been exploring this field, including experimenting with VJing and projection setups, so I can better understand how visuals can respond to rhythm and physical context. I am currently learning different videomapping software and developing my technical understanding of projection design. This process is still ongoing, but it is helping me build a foundation for working with spatial storytelling and playing with combining different media in one project. While the formats vary, each project requires balancing research, visual hierarchy, and storytelling. Together, they reflect my interest in how ideas travel from concept to communication, and how visual design can shape the way they are received and experienced.

Category Videomapping, Spatial Design, Projection-Based Visuals
Year 2026
Tools PremierPro, Resolum Arena

Across these projects, I worked as a researcher and visual designer. My responsibilities included developing conceptual frameworks, testing projection approaches, designing motion-based visuals, and translating ideas into spatial and time-based experiences

These projects explore experimental approaches to videomapping and projection-based design. Each project began with a different concept or constraint, but all focused on translating ideas into spatial and time-based visual experiences through projection, movement, and atmosphere.

  • Live videomapping performance using Resolume, based on the conceptual connection of three given keywords into a real-time visual composition.
  • A project exploring different media forms, where static graphic work was translated into a spatial experience through light and projection.

These projects represent an ongoing exploration of videomapping and projection-based design. This field has become a significant source of inspiration in my practice. Working with light, space, and movement has opened new ways of thinking about how visual communication can extend beyond static formats.

Through these experiences, I began to see the potential of projection not only in performative or visual installations, but also in broader contexts such as exhibitions, spatial branding, and experiential marketing. It has expanded my understanding of how design can shape environments and influence the perception of content in real space.

Live Performance - Choice – Overload – Paralysis

Videomapping Performance

Choice – Overload – Paralysis is a videomapping project created from the challenge of connecting three self-selected words into a single visual narrative. Inspired by a personal experience familiar to many, the struggle of choosing what to watch in an age of endless streaming options, the project explores how limitless choices can lead to decision fatigue. By combining imagery from acclaimed cinema and popular reality television, the work visualizes the journey from intention to frustration and, ultimately, settling for the easiest choice. The video shown here is only an excerpt; during the presentation, I was behind the camera performing live VJing, mixing and manipulating visuals in real time.

Projection Design

Static to Spatial — The Individual

Mixed Media Project

The individual is a projection-based work exploring the relationship between the individual and the crowd. Developed as an assignment to combine analogue and digital media, the work began with observations of people moving through the city and a curiosity about the stories hidden within their lives. Using self-recorded video footage, graphic compositions, and hand-drawn interventions with oil crayons, I experimented with the interaction between static and moving images. The final outcome combines projection and physical drawings, creating an atmospheric dialogue between analogue and digital media while reflecting on our desire to both stand out and belong.
